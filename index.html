Who called, and what did

You are a biological anthropologist. Your phone rings. Someone has found fossilized primate bones and needs your expert analysis: how did this animal move, and how big was it? All you have are the bones — and 478 modern primate specimens to compare them to.

Your Mission

Analyze the bones. Build the case.

Using limb proportion data from hundreds of modern primates, you will measure the mystery specimen's bones, plot them against the comparative dataset, estimate body mass, and construct a scientific argument about how this animal moved through its environment.

There is no single right answer. The evidence will point in multiple directions. Your job is to reason through the tension, acknowledge what doesn't fit, and make the strongest defensible claim the data supports.

Primate Locomotor Patterns
🌿
Arboreal Quadruped
Moves on all fours through tree branches; forelimbs and hindlimbs roughly equal in length
IMI ~85–100
🦍
Knuckle-Walker
Walks on knuckles on the ground; long forelimbs relative to hindlimbs
IMI ~100–120
🤸
Brachiator
Swings below branches using forelimbs; dramatically longer forelimbs than hindlimbs
IMI ~120–160
🧍
Biped
Walks upright on two legs; short forelimbs relative to long hindlimbs
IMI ~60–75
🦘
Vertical Clinger & Leaper
Clings vertically to trunks and leaps between them; hindlimb-dominated like bipeds
IMI ~55–70
🏃
Terrestrial Quadruped
Moves on all fours on the ground; limb proportions reflect speed over climbing
IMI ~80–95
